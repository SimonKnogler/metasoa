## Brief Abstract (Up to 255 characters)
A perceptual decision-making task where you identify which shape you control with your mouse. Includes colored cues and ratings of confidence and control. Duration: 60 minutes.

---

## Detailed Description (Up to 15,000 characters)

In this experiment, you will complete a control detection task where two shapes (a square and a circle) move on the screen. Your goal is to identify which shape you are controlling with your mouse movements.

**What you'll do:**
- Watch two shapes move on screen and decide which one follows your mouse movements
- Press 'A' if you controlled the square, or 'S' if you controlled the circle
- Rate your confidence in your decisions
- Rate how much control you felt over the shape's movement

**Experiment Structure:**
The experiment consists of 5 blocks with rest breaks between them:
- Block 1: Practice trials with feedback
- Blocks 2-5: Main experimental trials with colored cues

**Important Details:**
- You must respond within 5 seconds on each trial
- Some blocks include feedback, others do not
- You will provide confidence and control ratings during test blocks
- Breaks are provided every 100 trials and between blocks

**Duration:** Approximately 60 minutes

**Requirements:**
- Must be completed on a computer with a mouse (no trackpad)
- Quiet environment recommended
- Full screen display required
